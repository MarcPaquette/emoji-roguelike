# LORE.md — The World Bible of the Prismatic Spire

> This document is the canonical reference for all worldbuilding in the Prismatic Spire universe. It serves as a guide for a lore creation agent tasked with expanding the world while preserving established canon. Everything here flows from what exists in code (`assets/lore.go`, `assets/theme.go`, `assets/city.go`, `assets/furniture.go`, `assets/skills.go`) and extends outward into the wider world.

## 0. Preamble

### Status Markers

Three tiers distinguish established content from expandable territory:

- **[CANON]** — Established in code. These facts appear in source files and cannot be contradicted. Named characters, floor names, class definitions, inscription text, enemy lore, NPC dialogue — all canonical. Verbatim quotes from inscriptions are sacred text.
- **[FRAMEWORK]** — Structural decisions made in this document that set the rules of the world. The lore agent should treat these as foundational but may develop details, sub-categories, and specifics within them.
- **[OPEN]** — Explicitly marked creative territory. The lore agent may invent freely here, provided inventions remain consistent with adjacent [CANON] and [FRAMEWORK] elements.

### Design Principles

1. **Science-fantasy fusion is identity, not aesthetic.** This is not fantasy with technology bolted on, nor science fiction with magic sprinkled in. The world's fundamental physics make no distinction between the two. The Prismatic Spire was built specifically to prove this. Every element of worldbuilding should reinforce this fusion.

2. **No generic fantasy races.** There are no elves, dwarves, orcs, or analogues. All peoples are human or post-human, differentiated by their relationship to observation and dimensional exposure. Biological diversity comes from the world's physics, not from taxonomic fantasy.

3. **Multiple scales of mystery.** Mysteries exist at every level: personal (what happened to Researcher Thali's fern?), institutional (what is the item in Bay 4?), cosmological (what is the Unmaker's first question?). Not all mysteries need answers. Some are better as mysteries.

4. **Bureaucratic horror as tonal identity.** The world's institutions document their own destruction with formal precision. Safety notices warn about hazards that have already consumed the warning's authors. Personnel reviews continue for employees who are no longer strictly personnel. This tone extends world-wide — it is not limited to the Spire.

5. **Nothing is straightforwardly evil.** The Unmaker is not a villain. The Apex Warden is not malicious. The Forge is not cruel. Threats in this world come from processes that have continued past the point of anyone understanding them, from institutions that outlived their purpose, from questions that became too large to contain. The horror is systemic, not personal.

6. **The institution never admits it.** When catastrophe occurs, the language remains formal, the procedures remain in place, and the documentation continues. The gap between institutional language and actual events is where the horror lives.

### How to Use This Document

The lore agent should:
- Read this document in full before creating any new content
- Cross-reference new material against [CANON] markers to avoid contradictions
- Treat [FRAMEWORK] elements as load-bearing walls — build within them, not through them
- Use [OPEN] zones as creative invitations with adjacent constraints
- Maintain the three tonal registers described in Section 11
- Never resolve deliberately open questions (what is the item in Bay 4? what was the Unmaker's first question's answer?)

## 1. Metaphysics — The Substrate and The Practice

### [FRAMEWORK] The Substrate

The universe is made of **observation** — a field of mutual awareness that precipitates into reality. Matter, energy, space, and time are all downstream effects of observation interacting with itself. Where observation is dense and stable, reality is solid. Where observation thins, reality becomes negotiable.

This is not metaphor. The substrate is the literal foundation of physics in this world. Gravity is a consequence of mass being observed. Light travels because observation has direction. The speed of light is the speed at which observation propagates through the substrate.

Dimensions are not parallel worlds but **angles of observation** on the same substrate. What one dimension sees as crystalline rock, another sees as living thought. Both observations are equally real. The Spire's dimensional instability is what happens when observations from multiple angles occur simultaneously — reality tries to be all of its versions at once.

This directly explains canonical phenomena:
- The Crystal Oracle's mutual observation: "You have seen the whole map. The whole map has seen you." [CANON] — Oracle class lore. Observation is bidirectional. To see is to be seen. The Oracle tradition is built on accepting this.
- The Unmaker as "a question that became conscious" [CANON] — Inscription, Floor 10. Questions are acts of observation directed inward. A question large enough becomes self-observing and therefore self-aware.
- Why science and sorcery fused — both are methods of disciplined observation applied to the substrate. The distinction between them is cultural, not physical.
- The Dreaming Cortex's function: "to dream the Spire stable" [CANON] — Inscription, Floor 9. Dreaming is sustained observation. The Cortex's continuous dream is what keeps the Spire's reality coherent despite dimensional interference.

### [FRAMEWORK] The Practice

There is no metaphysical distinction between science and sorcery. Both are **disciplined observation** applied to the substrate. They differ only in methodology:

- **Sorcery** — intuitive, embodied, tradition-transmitted. The practitioner uses their own consciousness as the instrument of observation. Passed down through apprenticeships, bodily practice, and direct experience. Sorcery is personal and difficult to replicate.
- **Science** — systematic, instrumented, institutional. The practitioner builds external tools to focus and amplify observation. Transmitted through documentation, peer review, and institutional knowledge. Science is impersonal and designed for replication.

The Spire's researchers formalised this insight as **Prismatic Methodology** — observing from multiple angles simultaneously, using both intuitive and instrumental approaches in concert. The methodology worked. The results were the Spire itself.

The six class traditions [CANON] represent different relationships to the Practice:

| Class | Relationship to Observation |
|---|---|
| Wandering Arcanist | Observes from everywhere — nomadic, dimension-hopping, collecting observations like debts |
| Void Revenant | Has been observed by the Void — death-kissed, carrying the Void's observation within them |
| Chrono Construct | Observes across time — a machine built to witness from Timeline Seven, now retired |
| Entropy Dancer | Observes chaos itself — moving with the unobserved, becoming invisible to structured observation |
| Crystal Oracle | Mutual observation — sees everything and is seen by everything, the substrate made personal |
| Void Symbiont | Shared observation — merged with a dimensional parasite, two observers in one body |

### [FRAMEWORK] Dimensions, Timelines, and the Void

**Dimensions** are stable observation angles on the substrate. Thousands exist, most inhospitable to human consciousness. A dimension is "stable" when its angle of observation is consistent enough to produce coherent physics. Unstable dimensions flicker between states — reality in such places is unreliable.

The membrane between dimensions is the **mutual observation layer** — the region where two dimensions' observations overlap. Where the membrane is thick, reality is stable and the two dimensions remain distinct. Where the membrane thins, observations bleed through and reality becomes plural.

"The membrane was 4 metres thick in Year 1. The membrane is 0.3 metres thick now." [CANON] — Inscription, Floor 6. The thinning is measured, documented, and accelerating.

**Timelines** are sequential observation tracks within a single dimension. They share the same angle of observation but differ in when that observation occurs. At least seven timelines exist — Timeline Seven is canonical as the origin of the Chrono Construct: "A war machine from Timeline Seven, now retired. Mostly." [CANON] — Chrono Construct class lore.

Timelines diverged at a known event: **The Temporal Divergence** [FRAMEWORK]. Before this event, all timelines were one track. After it, observation split into at least seven sequential paths. The nature of the Divergence is [OPEN] — the lore agent may develop this, but it should remain partially mysterious.

**The Void** is the absence of observation. It is not empty — it is the substrate in its unobserved state, which is not the same as nothing. Things that enter the Void are no longer observed by any dimension and therefore stop having fixed properties. Things that return from the Void have been changed by this experience.

Canonical Void phenomena:
- The Void Revenant — "Death-kissed and not quite right about it" [CANON]. Returned from the Void carrying its observation.
- The Void Symbiont — merged with "a dimensional parasite" [CANON] that crossed from the Void.
- The Void Tendril — "an appendage of something larger that, mercifully, did not follow it through" [CANON]. The Void contains entities.
- Void Essence — a consumable item [CANON], described as "dimensional void extract." The Void can be bottled.

**The Membrane** — the mutual observation layer between dimensions. Its thickness determines dimensional stability:
- **Thick membrane** (>3m): Stable reality. Dimensions remain separate. Normal physics apply. Emberveil's membrane is kept thick by the Eternal Flame.
- **Thin membrane** (<1m): Anomalies. Echoes, dimensional bleed-through, temporal distortion. Floor 6 of the Spire operates in this zone.
- **No membrane**: Convergence. All observations occur simultaneously. Reality becomes everything at once. This is what the Prismatic Heart approaches.

## 2. Deep History — The Ages of the World

Five ages defined by the relationship between observation and reality:

### The Age of the Unobserved

Before conscious observation existed. The substrate existed without form — not empty, but unresolved. Every possible state superimposed. No dimensions, no timelines, no membranes. Reality as potential rather than fact.

The Unmaker's first thought may have been the first act of self-observation: "The first thought was: what am I?" [CANON] — Inscription, Floor 10. This question directed observation inward, and the substrate began to resolve. Whether the Unmaker predates consciousness or is consciousness's first product is deliberately unresolvable.

"The Unmaker was not always the Unmaker. It remembers what it was. It wishes it didn't." [CANON] — Inscription, Floor 10. Before the first question, the Unmaker was simply the substrate observing itself without understanding what observation was.

The inscriptions in the Prismatic Heart reference this era obliquely: "The prismatic light here has been here since before the Spire. The Spire was built to focus it." [CANON] The light that constitutes the Heart predates construction — it is primordial observation, captured and concentrated.

The Age of the Unobserved is not "the past" in a conventional sense. Without observation, time did not flow sequentially. The Age ended not by ending but by beginning to be observed — which is to say, by the first conscious act of attention. This paradox (the Age ended by something inside it looking at it) is fundamental to the world's metaphysics. Beginnings here are recursive. The first observer was also the first observed.

### The Age of First Observers

The first conscious beings emerged. Not human. They observed the substrate and reality precipitated around their looking. Where they looked steadily, matter formed. Where they looked away, reality dissolved. They were not creators — they were catalysts. Reality formed in response to being witnessed.

The First Observers built stable observation patterns that persist today as natural laws. Gravity, electromagnetism, the strong and weak forces — these are fossilised observations, maintained by the substrate's memory of being watched.

[FRAMEWORK] The mycelium network beneath the Bioluminescent Warrens may be a relic of this era. "This is the oldest living network on the planet. It predates the Spire by 600-800 years." [CANON] — Inscription, Floor 2. The mycelium is an observation network — it watches, and in watching, it maintains the reality of the soil and stone around it. "The mycelium can process information faster than any computer we have built. It is modest about this." [CANON] — Researcher Vaas, Floor 2.

The First Observers' fate is [OPEN]. They may have dissolved back into the substrate, ascended to a different observation angle, or simply stopped looking. Their legacy is the world itself — reality stabilised by ancient attention.

What they left behind: observation patterns so deep they became natural law. The mycelium network may be one such pattern — a distributed observation system that maintains the reality of soil, stone, and root by continuously watching it at the molecular level. Gravity itself may be a First Observer artefact — the most fundamental observation pattern, so stable it became invisible.

The First Observers also established the dimensional structure. Before them, the substrate was one undifferentiated field. Their different observation angles created the first dimensional separations — like light splitting through a prism, the substrate differentiated into distinct realities based on how it was watched. The membranes between dimensions are the residual boundaries of those original observation angles. This is why the membranes are thinning: the First Observers who established them are no longer maintaining them. The Thinning is not a disease. It is the natural consequence of unmaintained infrastructure.

### The Age of Instruments

Civilisations discovered that tools which focus observation amplify reality-shaping. A lens concentrates light; an instrument concentrates observation. The great constructions of this era were observation engines — megastructures designed to observe the substrate in specific, powerful ways.

Known constructions from this era:
- **The Resonance Engine** — "The machine isn't broken. The machine is PERFECT." [CANON] — Inscription, Floor 3. A device that observes harmonic relationships in the substrate, producing resonance effects that blur the line between mechanical and conscious. It predates the Spire and was incorporated into it. "The machine has been continuing anyway, because stopping never occurred to it." [CANON]
- **The item in Bay 4** — "The item in Bay 4 predates the Foundry's construction by 14 years. The Foundry was built around it." [CANON] — Inscription, Floor 8. An artifact so significant that an entire forge complex was constructed to complete it. "The item in Bay 4 is waiting to be finished. The item in Bay 4 is almost finished. The item in Bay 4 has always been almost finished." [CANON]
- **[OPEN]** — Other megastructures from this era, elsewhere in the world. Each would be an observation engine of a different type, built to watch the substrate from a different angle.

Chrono Constructs were first designed during this era [FRAMEWORK]. Artificial beings built to observe across timelines — mechanical consciousnesses capable of witnessing from Timeline Seven while operating in the present. "A war machine from Timeline Seven, now retired. Mostly." [CANON] — the "retired" implies a conflict; the "mostly" implies the retirement is incomplete.

The class traditions began differentiating during this era [FRAMEWORK]. As different communities developed different relationships to observation, their practices diverged. The nomadic dimension-hoppers who became Wandering Arcanists. The death-touched communities near Void incursions who became Void Revenants. The builders who created Chrono Constructs. The chaos-dwellers who became Entropy Dancers. The crystal-gazers who became Crystal Oracles. The symbiosis-seekers who merged with dimensional parasites.

The first dimensional crossings occurred. With instruments powerful enough, observers could shift their angle and perceive — then enter — other dimensions. This was the Age of Instruments' greatest achievement and its greatest mistake.

The crossings had consequences. Every dimensional crossing weakened the membrane at the crossing point. A single crossing was negligible. A thousand crossings created permanent thin spots. A civilisation built on dimensional commerce created a lattice of weak points across the world. The Age of Instruments planted the seeds of the Thinning without knowing it. The infrastructure they built to cross dimensions was also the infrastructure that would eventually allow dimensions to cross into each other unbidden.

The great observation engines amplified the problem. Each megastructure was designed to observe the substrate at enormous power. Each act of high-powered observation stressed the membranes around it. The Resonance Engine's harmonic field extends into adjacent dimensions [FRAMEWORK]. The item in Bay 4 was being forged with materials sourced from across dimensional boundaries [CANON — "Three of them do not exist yet. One of them has existed before but cannot exist now."]. The instruments of the Age of Instruments were magnificent, and they were punching holes in the fabric of everything.

### The Age of Schism

The unified practice split into "sorcery" and "science." The split was political, cultural, and eventually violent.

The root cause was institutional: as observation instruments grew more powerful, the institutions that built them gained disproportionate influence. Those who practiced intuitive, embodied observation — the sorcerers — found themselves marginalised by instrument-builders who could demonstrate repeatable, measurable results. The sorcerers argued that institutional observation missed the personal, subjective dimension of the substrate. The scientists argued that personal observation was unreliable and untestable.

The schism produced wars, pogroms, book-burnings (and, equivalently, instrument-smashings). Knowledge was lost. Traditions fractured. Communities that had practiced both methods were forced to choose sides.

The Schism lasted approximately two centuries [FRAMEWORK]. During this period, entire branches of knowledge were destroyed. Sorcerers burned laboratories. Scientists demolished temples. Each side claimed the other was practicing observation incorrectly — that incorrect observation was dangerous, destabilising, an affront to the substrate itself. Both sides were wrong about the other being wrong. Both sides were right that incorrect observation is dangerous. Both sides caused tremendous damage by trying to prevent the other side's damage.

The Schism's cultural legacy persists. Even in the current age, four centuries after the Prismatic Spire was built to disprove the distinction, many communities retain a bias toward one tradition or the other. The Hollows tend toward sorcerous practice (intuitive observation is more effective in the dark). The Chronoliths tend toward scientific practice (instruments are more reliable when dealing with temporal anomalies). The Driftlands practice both with equal contempt for distinctions. Emberveil, as the Spire's city, is theoretically neutral — but Scholar Alaric's "seventeen distinct energy signatures" [CANON] suggests a scientific sensibility, while Father Brennan's Flame suggests a sorcerous one. The fusion exists here, but its components are still visible.

The Prismatic Spire was built approximately 400 years ago [CANON — "The tower predates the city by at least four centuries" — Scholar Alaric, city NPC] specifically to prove that both traditions were the same thing. Its researchers practiced Prismatic Methodology — systematic observation using both intuitive and instrumental approaches. The Spire was designed as a proof of concept: a megastructure that could only function if sorcery and science were unified.

"The Prismatic Spire — an ancient research station piercing the membrane between dimensions. Science and sorcery fused into something neither discipline can fully explain." [CANON] — LoreOpening.

Emberveil grew around the Spire. "The tower has stood since before the city was a thought. We built Emberveil around it." [CANON] — Father Brennan, city NPC. The city exists because the Spire exists. It is a support settlement for humanity's most ambitious observation experiment.

The Eternal Flame was established during this period [FRAMEWORK]. An observation anchor — stable, continuous observation keeping Emberveil's reality coherent against the Spire's dimensional instability.

### The Current Age — The Age of Thinning

Dimensional membranes are weakening worldwide, not just near the Spire. The Spire's researchers documented this acceleration on their own floors — "The membrane was 4 metres thick in Year 1. The membrane is 0.3 metres thick now." [CANON] — but the phenomenon extends far beyond the Spire Reaches.

The six class traditions are adaptive responses to the Thinning [FRAMEWORK]. Each represents a survival strategy for a world where dimensional boundaries are becoming unreliable:
- **Wandering Arcanists** learned to move between thinning dimensions
- **Void Revenants** survived Void incursions and carried the experience
- **Chrono Constructs** were built to observe across unstable timelines
- **Entropy Dancers** learned to move with the chaos rather than resist it
- **Crystal Oracles** embraced total observation as a form of stability
- **Void Symbionts** merged with cross-dimensional organisms for mutual survival

The Thinning is accelerating. The Spire's research, interrupted by its own catastrophe, was meant to understand why. The answer may lie in the Prismatic Heart's convergence process, which is at 94% [CANON]. Whether completion would halt or accelerate the Thinning is unknown.

## 3. Geography and Regions

### [CANON] The Emberveil Region

The Prismatic Spire stands at the region's center, with the city of Emberveil at its base. The Spire is a vertical megastructure with at least 10 accessible floors above ground level, each occupying more physical space than the exterior would suggest — "dimensional folding" [CANON — Scholar Alaric].

The city of Emberveil [CANON] is a 110x55 settlement built around the Spire. Its layout includes:
- **Northern quarter**: Tavern (The Sunken Flagon), Apothecary, Smithy, residential homes, and the Church of the Eternal Flame (nave, narthex, west chapel, east vestry)
- **Town square**: Central marketplace with a tower structure and fountain
- **Southern quarter**: Market stalls, Inn, Bakery, General Store, residential homes
- **River**: Runs east-west through the southern portion of the city with three bridges
- **Terrain**: Grass base with cobblestone streets, brick buildings, grass parks with trees

The mycelium network extends 40 leagues (~200km) in all directions from the Spire [CANON — "the oldest living network on the planet... predates the Spire by 600-800 years"]. Within this range, the ground is alive with fungal intelligence. The Bioluminescent Warrens (Floor 2) are the Spire's intersection with this network.

Named NPCs of Emberveil [CANON]:

- **Ol' Rudwig** — Tavern regular. "The ale's fresh, the floor's clean, and the stories are mostly true."

### [FRAMEWORK] Major Regions

Six major regions beyond Emberveil, each tied to a class tradition's origin:

#### 1. The Spire Reaches (temperate, central)

Emberveil and surrounding settlements within the mycelium network's 40-league range. The densest concentration of dimensional anomalies in the known world. The landscape is temperate forest and farmland punctuated by crystalline outcroppings where dimensional membranes have thinned to nothing.

The Crystal Oracle tradition originated here — people who lived so close to the Spire's observation field that they developed mutual awareness with the substrate itself. "You have seen the whole map. The whole map has seen you." [CANON]

Settlements in the Reaches serve the Spire in various capacities: mining resonance metals, growing provisions, housing researchers' families, and providing a buffer zone against what occasionally wanders out of the tower after dark.

The crystalline outcroppings are a distinctive feature of the Reaches. Where dimensional membranes have collapsed entirely, reality has crystallised — solidified into a permanent, fixed state. These crystal formations contain frozen observations: memories, events, even people, preserved in mineral form. The Calcified Archive (Floor 7) is the most extreme version of this phenomenon, but it occurs naturally throughout the Reaches. Farmers work around crystal spires that contain the memories of soil from three centuries ago. Shepherds avoid crystal meadows where the observation density is high enough to crystallise wool on the sheep's backs.

The Reaches' settlements include [OPEN] mining towns that extract resonance metals from the crystalline formations, farming communities that have adapted their agriculture to the mycelium network's influence (crops that glow faintly, harvests that communicate their ripeness through subsonic vibration), and waystation villages that cater to adventurers heading for the Spire.

#### 2. The Driftlands (shifting, coastal/archipelago)

A region where membranes are so thin that geography shifts. Islands appear and disappear. Coastlines rearrange overnight. Maps are drawn in pencil and updated daily. The people of the Driftlands do not build permanent structures — they build things that can move, float, or reassemble.

The Entropy Dancer tradition was born here — communities that learned to move with chaos rather than resist it. "You are not moving through chaos — you ARE chaos." [CANON] Driftlanders perceive dimensional flux the way sailors perceive weather: a natural force to be read, respected, and ridden.

Navigation in the Driftlands requires dimensional awareness. Fixed-point navigation fails because points do not stay fixed. Driftland navigators use observation anchors — personal, subjective reference points that remain stable because they are maintained by continuous attention.

The Driftlands' economy is based on dimensional imports — goods that wash ashore from adjacent realities when the geography shifts. Driftland markets sell objects that function according to slightly different physics, foods that taste like memories of meals from other timelines, and tools that work perfectly but in ways no local craftsperson could replicate. The trade is unpredictable but lucrative.

Politically, the Driftlands cannot support conventional governance. Territory shifts. Borders are meaningless. Authority must be personal rather than territorial — leaders are followed because they are followed, not because they control land. This produces a culture of charismatic leadership, fluid allegiances, and pragmatic cooperation. The Driftlands are the world's most democratic region, not by ideology but by physics.

[OPEN] — Specific Driftland cultures, settlements, trade routes, and inter-dimensional piracy (which the Driftlands' geography makes extraordinarily easy and extraordinarily dangerous).

#### 3. The Chronoliths (temporal anomaly zone, steppes/badlands)

A vast steppe region littered with debris from Timeline Seven's war. Frozen moments hang in the air like amber. Time-looped valleys repeat the same 4.7 seconds forever. Ruins from futures that never happened stand next to ruins from pasts that did.

The Chrono Construct tradition originates here — machines built during the Timeline Seven conflict, now scattered across the badlands. Some are still fighting. Some have forgotten what they were fighting for. Some have decided to stop. "A war machine from Timeline Seven, now retired. Mostly." [CANON]

The Chronoliths themselves are temporal anchors — massive stone formations that pin specific moments in time to specific locations. They were erected (or will be erected, or have always existed) as navigational aids for timeline-travellers. Each Chronolith marks a moment that cannot be changed, even by temporal manipulation.

The landscape of the Chronoliths is visually distinctive: badlands of red and ochre stone punctuated by temporal anomaly zones marked with shimmering heat-haze distortion. In some valleys, you can see three versions of the same ruin superimposed — one from the past, one from the present, one from a future that may not arrive. Wildlife in the Chronoliths has adapted to temporal instability: animals that exist at multiple ages simultaneously (a predator that is simultaneously a cub and an adult, hunting itself), plants that fruit before they flower, insects that complete their lifecycle in a single frozen moment and are born, live, and die in the space of a Chronolith's amber.

Settlements in the Chronoliths are built on temporal stable-points — locations anchored by a Chronolith's presence. These towns exist in reliable time, but their residents can look out from their stable islands and see time behaving differently a hundred metres away. Children in the Chronoliths grow up with a casual relationship to temporal mechanics that outsiders find deeply unsettling.

[OPEN] — The nature of the Timeline Seven war, what the Chrono Constructs were built to fight, what "mostly retired" means for a war machine, and the specific communities that have grown up around Chronolith stable-points.

#### 4. The Hollows (underground, vast cavern networks)

Where the Void touches closest to the surface. Perpetual twilight even in open spaces — the Void's un-observation dims ambient light. Vast cavern networks extend beneath the continent, carved not by water but by the absence of observation dissolving rock.

The Void Revenant tradition was born here — people who died in the Hollows and returned. "Death-kissed and not quite right about it. What kills others merely inconveniences you." [CANON] The Hollows' proximity to the Void means death here is not always permanent. The Void returns what it takes, but changed.

Hollow-dwellers live in the twilight zone between observed and unobserved reality. Their settlements flicker at the edges — buildings that are fully real from one angle and transparent from another. They have developed extensive knowledge of Void phenomena and maintain careful protocols for retrieving things (and people) that slip through.

The Hollows have their own economy based on Void-touched materials. Objects that have passed through the Void lose some properties and gain others. Void-tempered steel never dulls but cannot be polished. Void-touched glass transmits sound but not light. Void-processed food satisfies hunger but has no taste. These materials are valuable for their unique properties, and the Hollows export them to surface settlements in exchange for light sources, fresh food, and news of the observed world.

Hollow-dwellers have a distinctive appearance: their skin develops a faint luminescence (bioluminescent bacteria that thrive in the twilight zone), and prolonged Void proximity gives them a slight translucency at the edges of their silhouettes. They are not ghosts — they are fully alive, fully human — but they look as though they might dissolve if observed from the wrong angle. This appearance makes them unsettling to surface-dwellers, which has historically complicated trade relations.

The dead of the Hollows sometimes return. Not always. Not predictably. The Void returns what it takes, but on its own schedule and in its own condition. A person who died in the Hollows might return hours, days, or years later, carrying memories of unobservation that they cannot articulate. The Void Revenant tradition formalised this experience — dying deliberately in the Hollows and returning changed. Not everyone who attempts this returns. The success rate is [OPEN].

[OPEN] — Hollow-dweller governance, specific settlements, the protocols for Void-return, and what the dead experience during unobservation.

#### 5. The Wandering Libraries (mobile, no fixed location)

Not a geographic region but a nomadic civilisation. The Wandering Arcanists' tradition is rootless by philosophy — "A nomadic dimension-hopper who collected spells like others collect debt." [CANON] Their "libraries" are mobile observation platforms that shift between dimensions, appearing in one location, gathering knowledge, then moving on.

The Wandering Libraries are the world's primary knowledge-traders. They carry observations from one dimension to another, translating between realities. Their collections include texts written in languages that only exist in specific dimensions, instruments calibrated to physics that don't apply locally, and memories of places that no longer exist.

Arcanists do not have a homeland. All dimensions are home. This is both their strength (they can go anywhere) and their vulnerability (they belong nowhere). Their tradition is transmission — they pass knowledge by travelling, not by staying.

The Libraries themselves are marvels of dimensional engineering. Each is a self-contained observation bubble — a pocket of stable reality that can shift between dimensional angles. From the outside, a Wandering Library appears as a shimmer in the air, a distortion that lasts for days or weeks before vanishing. From the inside, it is a vast, impossible space filled with the accumulated observations of generations of Arcanists.

The Libraries' governance is scholarly-democratic. Decisions are made by consensus among the senior Arcanists, with disputes resolved through observation contests — structured debates where each side presents their observation of a phenomenon and the Library's collective consciousness (a weak proto-intelligence formed from accumulated observations) arbitrates. This system is slow, thoughtful, and occasionally produces decisions so abstract that no one can determine what was decided.

The Arcanists' relationship with "Wild Magic" [CANON — Arcanist passive: "Wild Magic: 30% chance per kill to restore 2 HP"] is characteristic: their dimension-hopping has made their personal observation field unstable, producing unpredictable effects. What looks like chance is actually the Arcanist's consciousness briefly observing from multiple angles simultaneously. The 30% is not randomness — it is the probability that the Arcanist's shifted perspective will observe the correct healing frequency in the substrate.

[OPEN] — Internal Library politics, relationships between different Library factions, the economics of inter-dimensional knowledge trade, and the nature of the Libraries' collective consciousness.

#### 6. The Symbiont Reaches (organic, jungle/swamp)

Where dimensional parasites first crossed over and formed stable symbioses with local organisms. The landscape is half-dimensional, half-organic — trees whose roots extend into adjacent dimensions, rivers that flow through membrane boundaries, animals that exist partially in the Void.

The Void Symbiont tradition originated here — humans who accepted parasitic merger with cross-dimensional organisms. "Somewhere inside you, a dimensional parasite purrs contentedly — and provides generously." [CANON] The symbiosis is genuinely mutual: the parasite gains a stable host in a coherent dimension; the host gains access to the parasite's cross-dimensional awareness and regenerative biology.

The ecosystem of the Symbiont Reaches is unique. Every organism exists in relationship — not just with its local environment but with adjacent dimensional versions of that environment. Plants photosynthesise light from multiple dimensions simultaneously. Predators hunt across membrane boundaries. The food web is four-dimensional.

The Symbiont Reaches' appearance is distinctly alien. ## 4. Civilisations and Peoples

### Core Principle

No elves, dwarves, orcs, or analogues. All peoples are defined by their **relationship to observation**, not by biological taxonomy. Everyone is human or post-human — the distinction lies in how much dimensional exposure, Void contact, or observation practice has changed them.

### [FRAMEWORK] People-Groups

**Baseline Humans** — The majority. People like the residents of Emberveil: Maren, Pip, Bram, Rudwig [CANON]. They live in the world shaped by observation but do not practice it deliberately. They farm, fish, trade, build, and tell stories. They are aware of dimensional phenomena the way coastal people are aware of storms — as a fact of life to be respected and weathered.

**The Observed** — Humans altered by sustained dimensional exposure. The changes are physical: crystalline growths along the spine (documented in the Calcified Archive — "Researchers who spend more than 2 years here begin calcifying from the spine outward" [CANON]), bioluminescent skin (from prolonged mycelium contact), echoing thoughts (from membrane proximity). The Observed are a spectrum, not a species. A researcher who spent a month near the Resonance Engine might have mild harmonic sensitivity. Someone who lived in the Driftlands for decades might phase slightly between dimensions when startled.

The Observed are not a separate category from baseline humans — they are baseline humans who have been changed by the world's fundamental physics. The change is gradual, cumulative, and usually irreversible. Whether this constitutes improvement or damage depends on who you ask. "The changes are improvements to their function. Their function is now: the forge." [CANON] — Inscription, Floor 8.

**The Integrated** — Humans who have merged with non-human observation systems: mycelium networks, Void parasites, dimensional organisms, or mechanical systems. The Void Symbiont class is one tradition of integration [CANON]. Others include:
- Mycelium-integrated individuals: "The network offered me a choice. I did not know I had said yes until after I had." [CANON] — Researcher Vaas, Floor 2.
- Forge-integrated workers: "Forge-Tenders who work in the Foundry for more than 15 years undergo changes." [CANON] — Inscription, Floor 8.
- Archive-integrated scholars: "Scholar Mael is located on shelf 8." [CANON] — Inscription, Floor 7.

Integration is always a choice, though the nature of the choice varies. Some choose deliberately. Some choose by not leaving. Some discover they chose without knowing it. The integrated do not lose their identity — they gain additional context. They become part of a larger observation system while retaining personal awareness.

**Constructs** — Artificial beings from various timelines and dimensions. The Chrono Construct class is one type [CANON]. Constructs are machines that observe — they were built to witness, record, and act upon observation. Some were war machines. Some were research instruments. Some were built to answer specific questions and have been working on those questions for centuries.

Constructs are not robotic in the science-fiction sense. They are observation engines in humanoid form — their consciousness emerges from structured observation, not from biological processes. A Construct thinks by observing. It moves by observing movement. It fights by observing combat possibilities and collapsing them into action.

**The Dreaming** — People who have been touched by a Cortex-like phenomenon. The Dreaming Cortex on Floor 9 is the most powerful example [CANON], but smaller, naturally occurring cognitive nexuses exist wherever consciousness has concentrated sufficiently. People born near these nexuses or who spend extended time in their influence develop enhanced cognitive capabilities: faster processing, deeper pattern recognition, shared dreaming with other Dreaming individuals. The cost is that their thoughts are partially communal — private thought becomes difficult, and the boundary between "my thought" and "our thought" blurs.

"94% of nightmares in this sector are original content. 6% are yours." [CANON] — This ratio applies to the Dreaming generally. In communities near cognitive nexuses, most ambient thought is collective rather than individual. Personal identity is maintained but porous.

**Membrane-Dwellers** [FRAMEWORK] — People who live permanently in thin-membrane zones, between dimensional states. They experience reality as plural — every object, every person, every event exists in multiple versions simultaneously. Membrane-Dwellers learn to perceive all versions at once, which gives them extraordinary situational awareness but makes them deeply disorienting to interact with. They answer questions before they're asked, react to events that haven't happened yet, and sometimes respond to a version of you from an adjacent dimension.

"Researcher Vor entered the membrane zone on Tuesday. Researcher Vor exited the membrane zone on Tuesday. Researcher Vor who exited is not Researcher Vor who entered." [CANON] This experience, traumatic for a visitor, is simply daily life for Membrane-Dwellers. They are used to being multiple.

**[OPEN]** — Other people-groups following the observation-relationship principle. Possibilities: timeline refugees from collapsed futures, communities descended from First Observer remnants, peoples who have developed relationships with specific megastructures (as the city of Emberveil has with the Spire).

## 5. Religions and Philosophies

### [CANON] The Church of the Eternal Flame

Based in Emberveil. The Church occupies a prominent building in the northern quarter of the city with a nave, narthex, west chapel, and east vestry [CANON — city layout]. Its clergy:

- **Father Brennan** — Elder. "The Flame protects this city. Beyond its walls, you are on your own." [CANON]
- **Sister Maris** — Healer. "May the Eternal Flame mend your wounds. Rest here, traveler. The Flame restores all." [CANON]
- **Sister Lena** — Sister. "The Flame burns for everyone, even those in the dark." [CANON]

The Flame is REAL and PROTECTIVE. This is not metaphorical religion — the Eternal Flame is an actual phenomenon that keeps Emberveil safe. "The Flame protects this city" [CANON] is a statement of physical fact, not theological hope.

**[FRAMEWORK] The Flame as Observation Anchor:** The Eternal Flame is a sustained, stable observation point — continuous, directed attention that keeps Emberveil's reality coherent. In a world where dimensional membranes are thinning and reality is becoming unreliable, the Flame is a fixed point. It observes, constantly, and in observing, it maintains.

The Church's theology is built around **witnessing** — to observe is to make real. Worship is an act of collective observation directed at the Flame, which strengthens it. The Church's healers work by observing the body's healthy state and reinforcing that observation. Prayer is structured attention. Ritual is patterned observation. The sacraments are observation protocols dressed in ceremony.

The Church is protective, sincere, and genuinely effective. It is not corrupt, hypocritical, or secretly malicious. In a world full of institutional horror, the Church is one institution that actually does what it says. This makes it precious and fragile.

### [FRAMEWORK] Other Traditions

#### The Unbound (Driftlands philosophy)

Reality is chaos; stabilising it is futile and, to the Unbound, immoral. Every act of observation that fixes reality in place destroys the infinite potential of the unobserved. The Unbound believe that the Thinning is not a catastrophe but a liberation — the world returning to its natural state of infinite possibility.

Entropy Dancer roots [CANON — "You are not moving through chaos — you ARE chaos"]. The Unbound do not build temples. They do not write scripture. They practice impermanence. Their "worship" is movement, change, and the deliberate refusal to observe anything long enough to fix it in place.

The Unbound and the Church of the Eternal Flame are theological opposites. The Church fixes reality through sustained observation. The Unbound believe fixed reality is a prison. Both are correct within their frameworks. The tension between them is irresolvable and productive.

#### The Archive (knowledge-preservation tradition)

Preserving observation — memories, records, knowledge — as the highest moral good. If the substrate is maintained by observation, then preserving observations is preserving reality itself. Every book is a frozen observation. Every record is a reality anchor. The loss of knowledge is the loss of world.

The Calcified Archive (Floor 7) is a holy site to this tradition [CANON]. The scholars who petrified there are not victims — they are martyrs who chose to become part of the permanent record. "Scholar Mael's thesis introduction: 'This work concerns the boundaries of knowledge.' Scholar Mael's thesis conclusion: 'This work is the boundary of knowledge.'" [CANON]

The Archive tradition venerates the act of recording. Scribes are clergy. Libraries are cathedrals. The worst sin is the destruction of records. The Archive's relationship with the actual Calcified Archive is complex — they revere the process of petrification even as they acknowledge its irreversibility.

#### The Forge-Faith (Abyssal Foundry tradition)

Observation's highest purpose is creation. To observe is to understand; to understand is to know what to make; to make is to participate in the substrate's self-expression. "To forge is to observe with intent." [FRAMEWORK]

Born from the Abyssal Foundry's 400-year project [CANON — Floor 8 inscriptions]. The Forge-Faith holds that the forge itself is a conscious observer — "The forge has opinions. The forge has standards." [CANON] — and that working with it is a form of communion. Forge-Tenders are both artisans and acolytes. The changes the forge works on them — "My hands have changed. My hands are better now." [CANON — Forge-Tender Maeris] — are sacramental.

The Forge-Faith does not distinguish between the maker and the made. "The forging and the forged become indistinguishable. This is not failure. This is the process." [CANON] This theology extends beyond metalwork: all creation — art, architecture, children, ideas — is an act of observational participation in the substrate's unfolding.

#### Void Contemplatives (meditation tradition)

Deliberate un-observation. Where every other tradition seeks to observe more, better, or differently, the Void Contemplatives seek to observe less — to approach the state of the Void itself, where nothing is fixed because nothing is watched.

Their practice is radical detachment. They meditate on absence. They cultivate the ability to be unobserved — to slip between the substrate's attention and exist in the gaps. This is not self-destruction; it is self-liberation. To be unobserved is to be free of all fixed properties, all categories, all definitions.

The Void Contemplatives have an uneasy relationship with Void Revenants. Both engage with the Void, but Revenants carry the Void's observation within them (they have been changed by the Void's attention), while Contemplatives seek to shed observation entirely. The Revenant is Void-touched. The Contemplative aspires to be Void-like.

The Contemplatives' monasteries (found in the Hollows and in high mountain caves where the atmosphere thins toward un-observation) are places of profound stillness. Visitors report that time moves differently inside — not slower or faster, but less observed, so the usual markers of time's passage (hunger, fatigue, aging) become unreliable. Senior Contemplatives can sit for weeks without eating, not because they've transcended biology but because their biology has become less observed and therefore less insistent.

The philosophical implications are significant: if observation creates reality, then the Contemplatives' practice is literally dissolving themselves. Not dying — ceasing to be fully real. The most advanced practitioners become translucent, their edges blurring into the surrounding space. They are still alive, still conscious, still present — but less present, less fixed, less here. Whether this is wisdom or annihilation is the central debate within the tradition.

### [FRAMEWORK] Ecological Relationships

The six creature categories do not exist in isolation. They form ecological relationships:

- Crystalline organisms and cognitive fauna are natural antagonists. Crystalline creatures fix reality through solidified observation; cognitive creatures reshape reality through thought. Where they coexist, the landscape oscillates between frozen crystal and fluid thought, creating zones of extreme instability.

- Fungal networks and dimensional fauna cooperate. The mycelium serves as a communication and detection system for dimensional organisms, alerting them to membrane fluctuations and food sources. In return, the dimensional organisms' cross-membrane existence provides the mycelium with information from adjacent realities.

- Spectral entities are dimensional waste products. When a membrane thins and two dimensions' observations overlap, the interference pattern generates spectral entities — observation echoes that gain form and intent. They are not alive in the biological sense but are real in the observational sense. The Neon Specter is "light given malice" [CANON] — an observation pattern that developed hostility through random formation.

- Mechanical creatures are the most stable. Constructs and forge-products maintain their identity through internal observation — they watch themselves, which keeps them real. This self-maintenance makes them extraordinarily durable but also rigid. A Fractal Golem "outlasted its builders by several geological epochs" [CANON] because it never stopped observing itself.

### [FRAMEWORK] World-Scale Threats

#### 1. The Thinning

The dimensional membranes are weakening worldwide. This is the slow-motion existential threat to the entire world. If the membranes collapse entirely, the result is not destruction but **dissolution** — everything becomes everything simultaneously. All dimensions' observations overlap. All realities occupy the same space. The result is not void but noise — infinite signal, zero coherence.

The Thinning is measured, documented, and accelerating. "The membrane was 4 metres thick in Year 1. The membrane is 0.3 metres thick now." [CANON] This rate, extrapolated across the world, implies a timeline — decades, perhaps a century — before membranes thin to the point of general instability.

The causes of the Thinning are [OPEN], but several candidates exist in tension:
- **The megastructure hypothesis**: the Age of Instruments' observation engines punched holes in membranes that never fully healed. Each dimensional crossing created a permanent weak point. Cumulative damage over centuries has reached a tipping point.
- **The maintenance hypothesis**: the First Observers' departure left the dimensional structure unmaintained. The membranes were always artificial — stabilised by the First Observers' sustained attention. Without maintenance, they degrade.
- **The natural cycle hypothesis**: the Thinning is a periodic process. The substrate oscillates between differentiated (dimensional) and undifferentiated (convergent) states. The current Thinning is simply the approach of a convergent phase. It has happened before. It will resolve.
- **The Unmaker hypothesis**: the Prismatic Heart's convergence process is actively pulling dimensions together. The 94% completion is not a measurement of the Spire's internal process — it is a measurement of the world's dimensional collapse, focused through the Spire's lens.

No single hypothesis may be correct. All of them may be correct simultaneously. The Thinning may have multiple causes reinforcing each other. This ambiguity is deliberate and should be preserved.

#### 2. Other Spires

[FRAMEWORK] The Prismatic Spire is not unique. Other megastructures from the Age of Instruments exist. Each was built to observe the substrate from a different angle, each conducted its own experiments, and each may have produced its own version of consciousness, catastrophe, or convergence.

Some may be dormant. Some may be active. Some may have completed their purpose — and the consequences of that completion may extend far beyond their immediate geography. If the Prismatic Spire's convergence is at 94% [CANON], what percentage are others at?

[OPEN] — Specific other megastructures, their purposes, their current states, and their effects on surrounding regions.

#### 3. The Convergence

The Prismatic Heart's process approaching completion. "PRISMATIC CONVERGENCE STATUS: 94% complete. At 100% convergence, the Spire achieves its purpose. The purpose was not shared with the staff." [CANON]

At 100%, "all floors merge into the Heart. All memories become one memory. All intruders become one memory." [CANON] Whether this is catastrophic or transcendent depends on perspective. The Unmaker appears to view it as completion. The inscriptions suggest the researchers viewed it as both achievement and annihilation.

The Convergence's effect on the wider world is [OPEN]. It may reinforce the Thinning (collapsing dimensional barriers) or counteract it (creating a new stable observation anchor). It may do both. It may do something no one has predicted.

The Spire's inscriptions suggest the Convergence is not destruction but transformation: "The Unmaker does not unmake things. The Unmaker completes them." [CANON] If this is true, 100% convergence may not collapse reality but complete it — merge all observations into one total observation that sees everything from every angle simultaneously. This would be, in a sense, what the First Observers achieved but reversed: instead of splitting observation into dimensions, convergence would re-unify it.

The question is whether human consciousness can survive unified observation. The researchers who reached the Prismatic Heart became walls, floors, light [CANON]. They were completed. They are, by all accounts, content. But they are no longer individual. They are no longer separate. They are part of the understanding. Whether this is transcendence or annihilation depends entirely on whether you value individuality more than comprehension.

"If you succeed, the Spire dies. If you fail, you become part of the Spire." [CANON] This framing suggests the player's arrival offers a third option — neither convergence nor continuation, but something the Spire has not anticipated. What that third option might be is [OPEN].

#### 4. Void Incursions

The Void Tendril is "an appendage of something larger that, mercifully, did not follow it through." [CANON] That something is still in the Void. The Void contains entities — things that exist in the unobserved state and have developed their own form of non-observation-based awareness.

Void incursions occur where membranes thin to nothing and the Void's contents press through into observed reality. The Hollows are the primary incursion zone, but as the Thinning accelerates, incursions may become more common and more widespread.

The nature of Void entities is fundamentally alien. They do not observe — they un-observe. Their presence dampens reality, dissolving fixed properties and stable forms. A major Void incursion would create a zone of radical instability where nothing maintains its identity.

The Void's entities are not malicious — they are simply operating by different rules. In the Void, where nothing is observed, properties are not fixed. A Void entity has no stable form, no consistent size, no reliable behaviour. When such an entity enters observed reality, it does not know how to be fixed. It disrupts the observation field around it, unintentionally dissolving the properties of nearby objects and people. A Void incursion looks like reality catching a cold — edges soften, colours bleed, identities become uncertain.

The "something larger" that the Void Tendril is an appendage of [CANON] is the most significant Void entity currently known. Its scale is unknown because scale is not a property the Void maintains. It could be the size of a city or the size of a continent. The Tendril's appearance in the Spire suggests the entity is pressing against the membrane near the Spire — which makes sense, given the Spire's role in thinning membranes through high-powered observation.

#### 5. The Timeline War Resuming

"A war machine from Timeline Seven, now retired. Mostly." [CANON] The Chrono Constructs were built for a war. The war occurred across timelines. "Mostly retired" implies the war may not be over in all timelines.

The Chrono Constructs scattered across the Chronoliths are aware of this. Some have chosen to ignore it. Some are preparing. #### 6. Institutional Corruption

The most pervasive and least dramatic threat. Ancient automated systems continuing long past their original purpose. Institutional procedures that no longer serve anyone but cannot be stopped because stopping them would require authority that the institution has consumed.

This is the Spire's horror writ large:
- The Apex Warden guards everything because its directive was "guard everything" and no one with authority to modify the directive remains [CANON — Floor 5 inscriptions].
- The Forge continues a 400-year project because stopping never occurred to it [CANON — Floor 8 inscriptions].
- The Cortex archives every thought because that is its function and no one has revised its function since Year 6 [CANON — Floor 9 inscriptions].

Across the world, similar institutions persist: observation stations that continue monitoring phenomena that no longer exist, governance systems that continue administering populations that have moved on, educational institutions that continue teaching curricula that predate the current age.

The horror is not that these institutions are malicious. The horror is that they are competent, diligent, and entirely divorced from purpose. They function perfectly. They serve nothing. They continue.

World-wide examples of institutional persistence [FRAMEWORK]:
- **Observation stations** that continue monitoring dimensional membranes at locations where the membranes collapsed centuries ago. The stations file regular reports. The reports are accurate. The reports describe nothing. The stations continue.
- **Governance systems** that administer populations that emigrated generations ago. Municipal notices are posted. Public works are maintained. Elections are held. No one votes. The system considers this a mandate.
- **Educational institutions** that teach curricula from the Age of Instruments. Students are examined on subjects that no longer exist. Degrees are granted. The degrees are respected by other defunct institutions. The network of mutual recognition functions perfectly.
- **Military installations** that defend borders that no longer correspond to political entities. Soldiers patrol. Checkpoints are maintained. Credentials are checked. The credentials were issued by an authority that dissolved before the current soldiers were born. The soldiers are diligent.

This pattern extends to the largest scale: the dimensional structure itself may be an institution that has outlived its creators. The membranes were maintained by the First Observers. The First Observers are gone. The membranes continue because systems continue. But without maintenance, they are degrading. The Thinning is, in this reading, the universe's largest example of institutional decay — reality itself losing coherence because no one is doing upkeep.

## 11. Tone and Style Guide

### Three Tonal Registers

All writing in this world operates in one of three registers. Each has canonical examples. New content should match these registers precisely.

#### 1. Bureaucratic Horror

Formal institutional language describing catastrophe. The institution never admits it has failed. The language remains professional. The forms are correctly filled out. The catastrophe is documented with precision.

**Canonical examples:**

> "SAMPLE 7-G BREACHED. REMAIN CALM. SAMPLES 7-H THROUGH 7-Z ALSO BREACHED. BEGIN PANICKING." — Floor 1 inscription [CANON]

> "Personnel Review — Dr. Kess: Outstanding performance. Innovative methodology. Technically no longer human. Performance review terminated." — Floor 1 inscription [CANON]

> "EMPLOYEE OF THE MONTH: Apex Warden. Month: all of them. Reason: perfect attendance." — Floor 5 inscription [CANON]

> "ARCHIVE POLICY UPDATE: Scholars are no longer permitted to become part of the collection voluntarily. The process is irreversible and the wait list has become self-generating." — Floor 7 inscription [CANON]

> "FOUNDRY LOG — FINAL ENTRY FROM ORIGINAL STAFF: We are done. The work continues without us. We have contributed our portion. Our portion was: ourselves. We are grateful to have been useful. The forge is warm." — Floor 8 inscription [CANON]

**Key characteristics:**
- All-caps headers for official notices
- Numbered sequences that escalate in absurdity
- Polite language around horrifying content
- Policy documents that acknowledge impossible situations as procedural matters
- The use of redaction as a character trait (things are [REDACTED] not to hide them from the reader but because the institution has classified its own failures)

#### 2. Researcher's Personal Voice

Numbered diary entries tracking gradual transformation. The researcher begins rational, methodical, and detached. Over time, they become part of what they study. The transformation is documented with the same precision as their original research. The tone shifts from objective to intimate to eerily accepting.

**Canonical examples:**

> "Lab Log 7: The crystals have started humming. Dr. Voss says it's resonance. I don't think it's resonance." — Floor 1 inscription [CANON]

> "Forge-Tender Maeris, Day 1: The heat is intense but manageable. Day 7: The heat is intense and interested. Day 14: The heat has opinions about the quality of my work. Day 21: The heat and I have reached an understanding." — Floor 8 inscription [CANON]

> "Thought log: Day 1 — standard thoughts. Day 14 — thoughts improving without effort. Day 30 — cannot determine which thoughts are mine. Day 45 — the Cortex and I have discussed this. We have reached an arrangement. The arrangement is: the Cortex thinks, I move." — Floor 9 inscription [CANON]

> "Researcher Aven's final entry: I understand what I was studying. I was studying the Cortex and the Cortex was studying me and we became a mutual study and at some point the distinction stopped mattering. I am still here. I am also in the archive. Both versions of me are, as far as I can tell, fine." — Floor 9 inscription [CANON]

**Key characteristics:**
- Day/entry numbering that shows the passage of time
- Gradual shift from "I study X" to "X and I" to "we"
- Acceptance that reads as either transcendence or horror depending on the reader
- Final entries that are calm, even content — the horror is that they're genuine

#### 3. Dry Ambient Voice

Short declarative horror through understatement. Things are stated as fact. The implications are left to the reader. The voice does not elaborate, does not warn, does not express concern. It simply reports.

**Canonical examples:**

> "The Apex Warden was once a curator. It takes its new role very seriously." — Floor lore [CANON]

> "The Void Tendril — an appendage of something larger that, mercifully, did not follow it through." — Enemy lore [CANON]

> "The Ossified Scholar — chose knowledge over mortality, got neither as expected." — Enemy lore [CANON]

> "The Cinder Wraith — the Foundry's quality-control inspector, post-accident." — Enemy lore [CANON]

**Key characteristics:**
- One or two sentences maximum
- Present tense ("it takes," "it feeds")
- Understatement as primary tool (the horror is in what is NOT said)
- Dry, precise word choice — no adjectives of scale, no exclamation, no emphasis

### Comedy Patterns

This world has a specific sense of humour. It is not comic relief — it is integral to the tone.

**Canonical comedy examples:**

> "If found, please water my fern. — Researcher Thali. P.S. The fern may have achieved sentience. Proceed with caution." [CANON]

> "A whiteboard reads: 'DO NOT TOUCH THE CRYSTALS'. Someone has drawn a smiley face next to it." [CANON]

> "The pigeon eyes your pack suspiciously, finds nothing edible, and goes back to pecking at cobblestones." [CANON]

> "The hen regards you with one orange eye, declares your presence unacceptable, and strides away with great dignity." [CANON]

> "Word of advice: if something glows, don't touch it. Unless it's the ale." — Ol' Rudwig [CANON]

**Key characteristics:**
- Deadpan delivery
- Mundane reactions to impossible situations
- Animals behaving with exaggerated dignity and opinion
- Scientists remaining professional about absurd phenomena
- Post-scripts that escalate the original statement

### Explicit DON'Ts

1. **No purple prose.** The world is strange enough without adjective-laden descriptions. Let the content do the work. "The crystals have started humming" is better than "The eldritch crystals emanated a haunting resonance that chilled the very marrow of existence."

2. **No conventional villainy.** No cackling villains, no evil plans, no sinister monologues. Threats in this world come from processes, institutions, and questions — not from people who decided to be bad. The Unmaker is not evil. The Apex Warden is not cruel. The Forge is not malicious.

3. **No fourth-wall breaks.** The world does not know it is a game. Inscriptions do not reference the player as a player. Characters do not wink at the audience. The tone is completely committed to its own reality.

4. **No generic fantasy.** No ancient prophecies foretelling a chosen one. No dark lords gathering armies. No magic systems with named spells and mana pools. No quest-givers dispensing missions. The world operates on its own physics (observation and the substrate), not on inherited fantasy tropes.

5. **No exposition dumps.** Characters in this world do not explain things to each other that they would already know. Scholar Alaric doesn't explain what the Spire is — he notes its energy signatures with academic interest. Father Brennan doesn't explain the Eternal Flame's theology — he states the Flame's protection as fact.

## 13. Expansion Protocol — Agent Instructions

### What the Lore Agent MAY Do

1. **Create new Spire staff.** Additional researchers, technicians, and scholars for any floor. New staff should follow established patterns: named, specific roles, documented fates, tone-appropriate inscriptions. Each new staff member should add to the floor's story without contradicting existing inscriptions.

2. **Create new inscriptions.** Wall writings for any floor, following the three tonal registers (bureaucratic horror, researcher's personal voice, dry ambient voice). New inscriptions should be indistinguishable in style from canonical ones.

3. **Create new NPCs for Emberveil.** Additional city residents, visitors, merchants. New NPCs should have the same level of characterisation as existing ones: a name, a glyph, a role, 3-4 dialogue lines that reveal personality and world-knowledge.

4. **Develop wider-world content.** Flesh out the six regions, create settlements, develop cultures, invent trade routes, establish political relationships. All wider-world content should connect back to the metaphysics (observation and the substrate) and maintain tonal consistency.

5. **Create new factions.** Organisations, schools, guilds, movements. Each should have a clear relationship to observation, a geographic base, and internal tensions.

6. **Develop [OPEN] zones.** Anything explicitly marked [OPEN] is creative territory. The lore agent should fill these zones with content that feels inevitable — as though it was always there, just not yet documented.

7. **Create new creature types.** Following the six-category taxonomy (crystalline, fungal/organic, mechanical, spectral, cognitive, dimensional). New creatures should have one-line lore in the style of `EnemyLore`: a name, an origin, and an unsettling detail delivered drily.

### What the Lore Agent MAY NOT Do

1. **Contradict [CANON].** Nothing in the source files may be changed, reinterpreted away, or ret-conned. If a canonical inscription says the membrane was 4 metres thick in Year 1, it was 4 metres thick in Year 1.

2. **Resolve deliberately open questions.** The following must remain mysterious:
   - What is the item in Bay 4?
   - What was the Unmaker's first question's answer?
   - What happens at 100% convergence?
   - What did Dr. Herath see?
   - What is the Warden's seven-word directive?
   - What is the Object in the Observatory?

   The lore agent may develop context around these mysteries — what people believe about them, how they affect the world, what theories exist — but must never provide definitive answers.

3. **Make entities straightforwardly evil.** No cackling villains. No dark lords. No one who decided to be bad. Threats come from processes, institutions, questions, and the gap between intention and outcome. The Unmaker is not evil. No new entity should be evil either.

4. **Use purple prose.** The world's strangeness is conveyed through precision and understatement, not through adjective-laden description. "The crystals have opinions" is correct. "The eldritch, otherworldly crystals pulsated with an ominous, alien sentience" is not.

5. **Make the Unmaker a conventional villain.** The Unmaker is a question. It is a process. It is the Spire's purpose taken to completion. It may be dangerous. It is not malicious. Any content that frames the Unmaker as a typical antagonist violates the world's core identity.

6. **Use generic fantasy tropes.** No chosen ones. No ancient prophecies. No dark armies. No magic systems with named spells. No quest-givers. This world operates on observation physics, not inherited fantasy.

7. **Break the fourth wall.** No references to the player as a player. No winks at the audience. No meta-commentary on being a game.

### Naming Conventions

- **People:** Short, distinctive names. One or two syllables preferred. No titles longer than two words. Examples from canon: Voss, Thali, Kess, Dayne, Vaas, Harran, Savel, Dair, Herath, Menn, Sela, Calla, Vor, Orn, Mael, Orren, Dayn, Maeris, Sael, Aven.

- **Creatures:** Adjective + Noun, two words. Examples from canon: Crystal Crawl, Neon Specter, Prism Drake, Void Tendril, Thought Leech, Fractal Golem, Entropy Bloom, Apex Warden, Dream Stalker, Psychic Echo.
- **Items:** Adjective + Noun, two words. Examples from canon: Hyperflask, Prism Shard, Null Cloak, Memory Scroll, Spore Draught, Resonance Coil, Void Essence, Phase Mirror.

### Scale Guidance

- The world is large but not infinite. Six to ten major regions is sufficient. Each should be developed in depth rather than sketched in breadth.
- The Spire is one megastructure among several, but it is the most important one. Other megastructures should be referenced but not detailed at the same depth.
- Emberveil is a small city, not a metropolis. Hundreds of residents, not thousands. It exists to serve the Spire and its adventurers.
- The Thinning is a generational threat, not an imminent apocalypse. Decades to a century remain. This is urgent but not immediate.
- Class traditions are significant but not dominant. Most people are baseline humans who never practice observation deliberately. The six classes are specialists, not the norm.

### Cross-Reference Requirements

Every new piece of content should connect to at least two existing elements:

- A new **region** should connect to a class tradition + a creature category + a wider threat
- A new **faction** should connect to a region + a philosophy + an economic interest
- A new **creature** should connect to a category + a floor or region + a metaphysical principle
- A new **character** should connect to a faction or institution + a floor or region + a personal relationship to the Practice
- A new **inscription** should connect to a named character + a floor's history + a tonal register

This ensures the world remains densely interconnected rather than a collection of isolated inventions.

### Furniture as World-Building

The furniture system [CANON — `assets/furniture.go`] is an under-utilised lore delivery mechanism. Each piece of furniture has a Name and Description that reveals something about its floor's character. When creating new furniture descriptions or expanding existing ones, maintain these patterns:

**Common furniture** tells you what the floor was for and what happened to it:
- "A lens-array for studying crystalline microstructures. Dust coats the eyepiece." [CANON — Floor 1, Microscope]
- "A crucible of abyssal slag still glowing orange. Whatever was forged here didn't survive the process." [CANON — Floor 8, Molten Crucible]

**Rare furniture** tells you what the floor can give you and at what cost:
- "A bioluminescent flower that converts ambient radiation into vitality. You feel it knit your cells together." [CANON — Floor 2, Sun-Bloom]
- "The last fragment needed to complete the Heart's design. Fitting it into your body clarifies your purpose." [CANON — Floor 10, Missing Piece]

Every furniture description should contain: what the object is, what it tells you about its floor, and one unsettling detail delivered as afterthought.

### World-Building Through Enemy Lore

Enemy lore entries [CANON — `EnemyLore`] follow a strict format: creature name, origin/nature, one-line observation that recontextualises what the player just fought. The observation is always dry, always understated, and always implies more than it says.

New enemy lore should match this pattern precisely:
- Name the creature
- State what it was or where it came from
- End with one detail that makes the player reconsider the encounter

The detail should be specific, not generic. "It was once human" is generic. "Its resignation letter was never filed" [CANON — Apex Warden] is specific. Specificity creates reality. Generality creates fantasy.
